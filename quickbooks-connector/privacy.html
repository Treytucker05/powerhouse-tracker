Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the PowerHouseATX QuickBooks Connector ("Connector") accesses and handles information when authorized to connect to a QuickBooks Online company.

1. Scope

The Connector is an internal tool used by PowerHouseATX and authorized operators. It is not offered as a public service and does not create public user accounts.

2. Information Accessed

When authorized through Intuit OAuth, the Connector may access QuickBooks Online accounting data made available through the Accounting scope. This may include company information, chart of accounts, reports, customers, vendors, invoices, bills, payments, purchases, deposits, journal entries, sales receipts, tax records, and other accounting records returned by QuickBooks Online APIs.

3. How Information Is Used

Information is used to support internal accounting review, tax-preparation, reporting, reconciliation, and workflow automation tasks requested by an authorized operator.

4. Storage

OAuth credentials for the Connector are stored locally on an authorized PowerHouseATX-controlled computer. The static website hosting this Privacy Policy does not collect or store QuickBooks data. QuickBooks data may be temporarily processed in local logs, local runtime memory, or local files created during operator-directed workflows.

5. AI and Automation Tools

The Connector may be used with local or operator-configured automation and assistant tools. If an authorized operator asks an assistant or model provider to analyze QuickBooks data, selected information may be processed by that configured tool or provider according to the operator's settings and the provider's applicable terms. Operators should not submit sensitive data to any tool or provider they do not intend to use.

6. Sharing

PowerHouseATX does not sell QuickBooks data and does not use QuickBooks data for advertising. Information is shared only as needed to operate the Connector, comply with law, or complete operator-directed workflows with authorized systems and service providers.

7. Security

PowerHouseATX uses reasonable local access controls for the computer and files used by the Connector. No method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, and users should protect local devices, credentials, and QuickBooks account access.

8. Revoking Access

QuickBooks authorization can be revoked through Intuit account settings or by removing the Connector's locally stored OAuth credentials. Revoking access prevents future API access but does not automatically delete local files or reports previously created at an operator's request.

9. Contact
